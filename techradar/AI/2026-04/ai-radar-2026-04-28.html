The Power of Power Law: Asymmetry Enables Compositional Reasoning

Natural language data follows a power-law distribution, where most knowledge and skills appear at very low frequency. Training under power-law distributions consistently outperforms uniform distributions across compositional reasoning tasks, such as state tracking and multi-step arithmetic. This is due to the beneficial asymmetry induced by power law sampling, which improves the pathological loss landscape and enables models to efficiently learn rare long-tailed skills with less training data. Power law sampling requires significantly less data complexity than reweighting or curating towards a uniform distribution.
