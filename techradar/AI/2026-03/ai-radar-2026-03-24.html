Latest Research Papers

cs.AI — Agents & Reasoning

HyperAgents: Recursive Self-Improvement Beyond Fixed Meta-Procedures

Extends Darwin Gödel Machine by allowing agents to improve not just behavior but the improvement procedure itself — enabling open-ended meta-level self-enhancement across coding, paper review, robotics reward design, and math olympiad grading.

ProMAS: Proactive Error Forecasting for Multi-Agent Systems

Addresses cascading failure fragility in LLM-based multi-agent systems using Markov Transition Dynamics to forecast errors before they propagate. A single logical fallacy can collapse entire long-horizon tasks.

AgenticGEO: Self-Evolving Agentic System for Generative Engine Optimization

Introduces a new optimization framework for LLM-based generative search engines, shifting from ranking-based retrieval optimization to content inclusion — a fundamentally different objective for SEO in the age of AI synthesis.

OpenSeeker: Democratizing Frontier Search Agents via Open Training Data

Demonstrates that strong search agents are primarily a data problem, releasing the full training pipeline and achieving competitive performance with limited data. An important step toward accessible frontier-quality search agents.

Me, Myself, and π: Evaluating LLM Introspection

Evaluates LLMs' ability to accurately introspect and report on their own internal states and reasoning processes, probing a fundamental question about model self-awareness and reliability.

AgentComm-Bench: Stress-Testing Cooperative Embodied AI Under Network Degradation

Benchmarks cooperative multi-agent AI systems under realistic network conditions — latency, packet loss, and bandwidth collapse — exposing how quickly communication failures degrade collective task performance.

FactorSmith: Agentic Simulation Generation via MDP Decomposition

Automates simulation environment generation using LLM agents that decompose tasks via Markov Decision Processes, with a Planner-Designer-Critic loop for iterative refinement — reducing manual environment engineering effort.

Domain-Specialized Tree of Thought via Plug-and-Play Predictors

Enhances Tree of Thought LLM reasoning by replacing generic tree structures with domain-specialized plug-and-play predictors, optimizing reasoning pathways for specific problem domains.

cs.LG — Models & Training

Nemotron-Cascade 2: Cascade RL + Multi-Domain On-Policy Distillation

A 30B MoE model with 3B active parameters achieving Olympiad-level reasoning by co-evolving RL and distillation across domains simultaneously. Demonstrates how smaller models can reach very high reasoning density.

Attention Residuals (Kimi Team)

Reworks residual connections in Transformer architectures — challenging a foundational design assumption that most treat as settled furniture — potentially improving information flow and model performance.

Mixture-of-Depths Attention

Enables attention across both sequence positions and layer depth simultaneously, directly attacking the feature dilution problem that emerges as Transformers grow deeper.

Efficient Reasoning on the Edge (Qualcomm)

Brings LLM reasoning to constrained edge devices by treating verbosity control, adapter routing, and KV-cache management as first-class engineering priorities — not afterthoughts.

MARLIN: Multi-Agent RL for Incremental Causal DAG Discovery

Uses multi-agent reinforcement learning to incrementally uncover causal graph structures from observational data, enabling more scalable discovery of complex causal relationships.

Collaborative Adaptive Curriculum for Progressive Knowledge Distillation

Proposes a collaborative adaptive curriculum for knowledge distillation that progressively increases difficulty — improving model compression efficiency compared to standard distillation approaches.

cs.RO — Robotics & Physical AI

JointFM-0.1: Foundation Model for Multi-Target Joint Distributional Prediction

Combines AI with stochastic differential equations to build a foundation model for joint probabilistic prediction of multiple targets simultaneously — useful for control and simulation in physical AI systems.

LLM-Enhanced Energy Contrastive Learning for OOD Detection in Graphs

Uses LLMs to improve out-of-distribution detection in graph neural networks that process text-attributed graphs, combining energy-based contrastive learning with language model representations.
