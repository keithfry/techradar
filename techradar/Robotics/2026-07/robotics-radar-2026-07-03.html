WaveLander: A Generalizable Hierarchical Control Framework for UAV Landing on Wave-Disturbed Platforms via Reinforcement Learning

Autonomous UAV landing on wave-disturbed marine platforms is challenging due to unpredictable motion and attitude variations. Existing methods struggle with either complex online optimization or limited interpretability. WaveLander introduces a novel, hierarchical reinforcement learning framework to address this difficulty. The approach separates the vertical landing decision from low-level flight stabilization control. Simulation results confirm that WaveLander achieves robust performance across randomized wave disturbances while generalizing effectively.

arXiv - cs.RO

The Three Dimensions of ROS 2 Middleware

ROS 2 utilizes middleware like DDS and Zenoh for distributed robotic communication, yet these systems face structural limitations in dynamic wireless settings. This paper proposes examining ROS 2's architectural limits using three dimensions: Space, Time, and State. Time relates to temporal predictability essential for control loops, while Space addresses abstracting physical topology for modularity. State management ensures contextual continuity despite node changes and connection interruptions. The work reveals structural trade-offs where achieving spatial abstraction or state continuity can compromise time-critical communication under wireless constraints.

arXiv - cs.RO

Neuro-Symbolic Safety Guidance for Vision-Language-Action Models via Constrained Flow Matching

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models' real-world use is hindered by insufficient safety measures beyond merely preventing immediate collisions. This paper introduces a neuro-symbolic guidance mechanism for flow matching based VLAs to enable predictive collision avoidance. The method frames safety enforcement as a minimum-norm constrained optimization problem applied during the trajectory denoising process. By analyzing and correcting predicted trajectories iteratively, it anticipates potential future collisions rather than just reacting to them. On the SafeLIBERO benchmark, this approach showed significant improvements, achieving 82.8% collision avoidance and better task success rates compared to single-step methods.

CommonRoad-Game: A Human-in-the-Loop Simulation Framework for Autonomous Driving

Introduces CommonRoad-Game, a lightweight human-in-the-loop simulation framework designed for testing autonomous driving motion planning algorithms with human participation. It addresses limitations of existing simulators by offering real-time interaction capabilities and being suitable for rapid prototyping. The framework features a multi-threaded architecture ensuring deterministic synchronization between simulated and real time. CommonRoad-Game integrates seamlessly with the CommonRoad platform, supporting scalable multi-agent simulations. Additionally, it includes a module to record driving logs, enabling the creation of diverse and reproducible test cases from human interactions.

arXiv - cs.RO

BIFROST: Bridging Invariant Feature Representation for Observation-space Sim2Real Transfer

Challenges in sim2real robot policy learning stem from mismatches between simulation and reality, which current methods address piecemeal. This work proposes BIFROST to exploit the shared structural basis underpinning task equivalence across domains. It introduces a shared history encoder trained using a cross-domain bisimulation objective on paired data. This objective ensures that observation-action sequences resulting in equivalent long-term behavior map close together in latent space, irrespective of domain differences. Empirical results confirm BIFROST enables zero-shot transfer for visual navigation and contact-rich manipulation where other methods fail due to combined visual or dynamics gaps.

arXiv - cs.RO

SE(2) Navigation Mesh

Proposes SE(2) Navigation Mesh (SE(2) NavMesh), a polygonal representation designed to capture yaw-dependent traversability for ground robots, overcoming limitations of traditional point clouds and meshes. It models traversable areas using footprint masks and builds a graph with explicit translational and rotational connectivity across different yaw layers. A novel pathfinding strategy, A*-String Pulling-A* (ASA), utilizes this mesh to hierarchically optimize robot position and heading. The method also includes an online procedure for incrementally updating the SE(2) NavMesh from real-time point cloud streams. Simulations show the SE(2) NavMesh captures significantly more traversable area, and its integration with ASA outperforms existing baselines in complex scenarios tested on physical hardware.

arXiv - cs.RO

A Reconfigurable Rocker-Bogie Robot for High Step Climbing and Turning

Proposes a reconfigurable rocker-bogie mechanism enabling efficient turning and high step-climbing with few actuators. The system can switch between four-wheel and six-wheel configurations by actively swinging bogies using motors at the joints. Omnidirectional wheels on the rockers allow for smooth, zero-radius turning modeled by differential drive in the four-wheel setup. Experimental results show the robot achieves this turning five times faster than conventional mechanisms, using only 17% of the average wheel torque. Furthermore, the prototype successfully climbed a 40 cm step in an average time of 6.4 seconds.

arXiv - cs.RO

Multi-Rate Nonlinear Model Predictive Control for Wall-Supported Bipedal Locomotion of Quadrupedal Robots

A novel layered planning and control framework using multi-rate nonlinear model predictive control (MR-NMPC) allows quadrupedal robots to achieve hybrid bipedal locomotion while supported by walls. This system dynamically plans both discrete contact points and continuous Center of Mass/orientation trajectories using a single rigid body dynamics model. The incorporation of explicit contact-point planning enhances dynamic stability over traditional heuristic foot placement methods. At the lower level, a nonlinear whole-body controller uses virtual constraints to track the MR-NMPC references across full-order dynamics. Simulations confirmed the robust wall-assisted locomotion on rough terrains, achieving a 2.9 times higher success rate than conventional MPC approaches.

arXiv - cs.RO

Path planning for unmanned naval surface vehicles

Addresses challenges in real-time collision avoidance for unmanned surface vehicles (USVs) against both fixed and moving obstacles. Proposes a system integrating a novel global path planner with a local path planner for robust navigation. The global planner combines Grassfire, a modified Grassfire, and an intuitive Probabilistic Roadmap variant to map obstacle-free paths from start to goal. For dynamic avoidance, the local planner uses advanced decision logic based on observed relative obstacle movement. Simulations validate this approach, showing superior performance compared to established methods like D*, particularly in achieving systematic routing behind moving objects.
