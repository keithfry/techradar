Neuro-Symbolic Drive: Rule-Grounded Faithful Reasoning for Driving VLAs

Neuro-Symbolic Drive is a new framework that improves Vision-Language Action (VLA) models by grounding their reasoning in explicit rules. It supervises the VLA using structured, rule-grounded reasoning traces extracted from classical rule-based planners. These traces detail the planner's internal decisions and are directly coupled to the executed trajectory during fine-tuning of Qwen3.5-4B. By deriving supervision this way, it ensures causal connectivity between the reasoning steps and the resulting motion planning. On benchmarks, this method significantly reduced error rates, such as decreasing ADE@3s from 0.47 to 0.26 with three cameras.

Data Augmentation: A Fourier Analysis Perspective

Data augmentation exploits known invariances by transforming training samples using a group action. Computational costs restrict using transformations from large groups entirely. This research proposes using Fourier analysis to investigate if sampling only a subset of these group elements retains the statistical benefits of full augmentation. Results demonstrate that for many classical learning problems, partial augmentation achieves the same minimax rates as full augmentation with vanishing error. Conversely, the study proves that achieving exact invariance through data augmentation necessitates averaging over the entire group, precluding use of strict subsets.
