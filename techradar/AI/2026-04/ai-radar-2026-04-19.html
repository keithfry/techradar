Parcae stable looping scales language models effectively

Proposes Parcae, a novel stable looped architecture designed to overcome instability issues in traditional looped models. Parcae achieves this stability by constraining the spectral norm of injection parameters using negative diagonal parameterization. The method allows for scaling model quality by increasing FLOPs via looping, an alternative to increasing parameters or data. Experiments show Parcae can achieve superior quality compared to large Transformer baselines under fixed parameter and data budgets. Specifically, Parcae improves core quality by 2.99 points up to 1.3B parameters while maintaining a fixed parameter count.
