A mini robot to simplify dental treatment

A miniature robot, called "MIR" (Miniature Intraoral Robot), has been developed at the University of Basel to simplify dental treatment by precisely preparing teeth for a crown. The robot measures 43x26x28mm and can be fitted comfortably into an open mouth, reducing the need for multiple appointments with dentists. Researchers have tested the robot's precision, finding it to be less than 0.2 millimeters in positional error, which will be further reduced after sensors are integrated into the system. The next step is to integrate sensors and a camera to enable monitoring of its position and progress during treatment.

HERMES: Heterogeneous Edge-Relational Multi-Head Embedded SSM Attention for Traffic Conflict Prediction at Signalized Intersections

HERMES, a heterogeneous edge-relational graph neural network with SSM-informed multi-head attention, was proposed for traffic conflict prediction at signalized intersections. Vehicles and pedestrians were represented as heterogeneous nodes, while vehicle-vehicle, vehicle-pedestrian, and pedestrian-pedestrian interactions were encoded as relation-specific edges. HERMES achieved an AUC-ROC of 0.9898, outperforming the strongest Transformer baseline and XGBoost, with a detection rate of 95.7% at a 5% false-alarm rate.

arXiv - cs.RO

PhysCoRe: Physics-Corrected Residual World Models for Material-Aware Deformable Dynamics

PhysCoRe presents a physics-corrected residual world model that combines a Material Point Method (MPM) simulator with two feed-forward neural networks. The design includes a material refinement module, which infers per-particle elasticity from visual observations, and a residual correction module, which learns the discrepancy between the simulator's internal dynamics and predicted corrections. This approach supports online material identification on novel objects and outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in prediction accuracy, providing a reliable distribution of confidence across an object's geometry. PhysCoRe achieves this by adapting its predictive uncertainty to steer further exploration toward regions where its estimate is least confident.

arXiv - cs.RO

Scalable Low-Cost Laboratory Automation: A Digital Twin-Integrated Robotic Platform for Autonomous Liquid Handling (RAINBOTTM)

A low-cost, openly reproducible liquid-handling robot called RAINBOTTM was developed by converting a consumer-grade Cartesian 3D printer. The robot features a precision single-channel pipette actuated through the printer's X--Y--Z gantry, with plunger and tip-eject motions controlled by Python. A browser-based digital twin synchronises with the physical platform in real-time, allowing remote monitoring and intervention, and is coupled to the CEID framework for goal-directed inverse-design search. The complete hardware costs under US$1300 establish an accessible physical--virtual framework for self-driving laboratory automation.

Researchers have developed a learning-based framework for safe and scalable multi-drone cooperative payload transport using Discrete Graph Control Barrier Function Proximal Policy Optimization (DGPPO). The framework introduces a minimal 2D abstraction that preserves task-relevant drone-payload coupling while remaining computationally efficient. A single learned policy generalizes across varying team sizes and task scenarios, and demonstrates safe operation in dynamic environments with other drone teams acting as obstacles.

Towards Capability-Aware Traversability Navigation for Unstructured Environments

Researchers propose Capability-Aware Traversability (CAT), a framework embedding physical limits into spatial feature space. CAT grounds dense supervision masks in physical trajectories through an interactive annotation pipeline and modulates semantic terrain maps with robot-specific traversability vectors using Spatially-Adaptive Denormalization (SPADE) blocks. On human-annotated and trajectory-aligned datasets, CAT leads all ranking-based metrics, improving AUROC by 11.0% and AUPRC by 15.8%.

arXiv - cs.RO

FELT: Generating Tactile Signals from Vision for Visuo-Tactile Manipulation

FELT, a learning-based framework, synthesizes per-finger pressure tactile images from RGB observations, reducing the need for tactile-equipped data collection. FELT uses a frozen visual encoder and a lightweight query decoder to predict tactile signals in a single feed-forward pass, capturing asymmetric contact patterns during interactions. At inference time, FELT only requires RGB data, allowing existing vision-only data to be augmented with tactile observations as generated tactile images or latent tactile features.

A real-time RGB-D perception pipeline for autonomous impact hammers in mining: self-filtering, rock segmentation and rock-breaking poses generation

A real-time RGB-D perception pipeline for autonomous impact hammers in mining simultaneously generates operationally feasible rock-breaking poses and a robot-free 3D representation of the workspace. The proposed approach combines image-based instance segmentation with geometric point cloud processing, operating on embedded hardware at approximately 10 Hz with a total latency of around 675 ms. This enables responsive closed-loop behavior when integrated with a control system. Experimental results in a representative scaled scenario demonstrate that the proposed system is suitable for real-time autonomous impact hammer operation.
