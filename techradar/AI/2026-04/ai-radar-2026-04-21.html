SetFlow: Generating Structured Sets of Representations for Multiple Instance Learning

SetFlow is a generative architecture that models entire MIL bags directly in the representation space using the flow matching paradigm combined with a Set Transformer-inspired design. The model captures intra-bag dependencies while being permutation-invariant and conditionally generates coherent sets of representations based on class labels and input scale. SetFlow was evaluated on a large-scale mammography benchmark, showing promising results that closely match the original data distribution and improve downstream performance.

A Mechanism Study of Delayed Loss Spikes in Batch-Normalized Linear Models

Batch-normalized linear models exhibit delayed loss spikes in training, which can be explained by normalization delaying instability through gradual increases in effective learning rate during stable descent. For whitened square-loss linear regression, explicit conditions are derived that bound the waiting time to directional onset and show self-stabilization within finitely many iterations. In logistic regression under restrictive assumptions, a finite-horizon precursor is proven. The study provides a stylized mechanism study of delayed loss spikes in batch-normalized models.

Extraction of informative statistical features in the problem of forecasting time series generated by It{\^{o}}-type processes

Researchers consider extracting informative features from time series generated by Itô-type processes without introducing additional information. They use parameters from mixture-type models to capture observed patterns and employ various reconstruction techniques. These techniques, including uniform and non-uniform methods, provide different types of reconstructed coefficients that account for dependence on the process value. The use of these additional features in autoregressive algorithms improves prediction efficiency compared to using only original time series data.

StrEBM: A Structured Latent Energy-Based Model for Blind Source Separation

StrEBM is a structured latent energy-based model for blind source separation that proposes assigning different latent dimensions their own learnable structural biases. Latent trajectories are optimized together with an observation-generation map and source-wise structural parameters, allowing distinct underlying components to evolve during training. The framework uses Gaussian-process-inspired energies with learnable length-scales but is intended as a more general structured latent EBM formulation. Experiments show that the model can recover source components effectively under linear and nonlinear mixing settings.

Symmetry Guarantees Statistic Recovery in Variational Inference

Variational inference (VI) uses a tractable family of distributions to approximate an intractable target density. Recent work has shown that symmetries of the target and variational family enable statistic recovery even under model misspecification, but existing guarantees are problem-specific. A new theory characterizes when variational minimizers inherit symmetries of the target and establishes conditions for identifying statistics. The framework unifies existing results and provides novel guarantees for directional statistics in von Mises-Fisher families on the sphere.

RHINO-AR: An Augmented Reality Exhibit for Teaching Mobile Robotics Concepts in Museums

RHINO-AR is an interactive Augmented Reality (AR) museum exhibit that reintroduces the historical mobile robot RHINO into its original exhibition environment at the Deutsches Museum Bonn. It combines real-time environment meshing with interactive visualizations of LiDAR sensing, traversability, and path planning to make robotics processes understandable to non-expert visitors. RHINO-AR was evaluated in a museum study with 22 participants, showing it was well received, effectively conveyed key navigation concepts, and generally preferred over the VR exhibit due to its stronger physical grounding and increased realism.

ICAT: Incident-Case-Grounded Adaptive Testing for Physical-Risk Prediction in Embodied World Models

Video-generative world models often downplay or omit key danger cues and severe outcomes, leading to unsafe preferences during planning and training on imagined rollouts. ICAT (Incident-Case-Grounded Adaptive Testing) proposes a solution by grounding testing in real incident reports and safety manuals. Experiments show that mainstream world models frequently miss mechanisms and triggering conditions and miscalibrate severity. ICAT-based benchmark results indicate the reliability required for safety-critical embodied deployment is not met.

Learning-Based Sparsification of Dynamic Graphs in Robotic Exploration Algorithms

A transformer-based framework trained with Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) is presented to prune graph structures during robotic exploration, limiting their growth and reducing redundant information. The framework achieves up to 96% reduction in graph size and produces consistent exploration across varied environments. Preliminary evidence suggests that the learned policy associates pruning decisions with exploration outcomes despite sparse reward signals. Intelligent pruning yields the lowest standard deviation of exploration rates compared to baselines.
