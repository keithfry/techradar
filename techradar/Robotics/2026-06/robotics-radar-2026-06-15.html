Occupancy-Grounded Room Segmentation for Hierarchical 3D Scene Graphs

Develops an occupancy-grounded approach for constructing hierarchical 3D scene graphs (3DSGs) used by indoor robots. This method anchors room nodes to tracked free-space regions derived from occupancy decomposition, assigning each room an explicit polygonal footprint. Evaluation across 12 Matterport3D scenes demonstrated that this anchoring recovers significantly more room instances compared to existing place-connectivity baselines. However, the gains come at a reduced precision cost. The study concludes that accurately modeling wall boundaries for room definitions remains an unsolved challenge for both presented methods.

arXiv - cs.RO

Scalable Dynamic Tactile Sensing Enabled by Passive and Flexible Acoustic Waveguides

Developed a passive, distributed system for dynamic tactile sensing using deep sub-wavelength acoustic waveguides. The structure employs elastic-membrane-capped Helmholtz resonators connected by spring-reinforced microtubes, maintaining constant acoustic transmission despite large bending. Embedding sparse microphones allows real-time signal localization and waveform reconstruction of low-frequency signals at high accuracy. A fast inference pipeline enables processing within 5.5 milliseconds. Prototypes demonstrate the technology's capability to sense diverse stimuli, from single hairs to particle impacts, establishing a scalable for next-generation interfaces.

arXiv - cs.RO

$\mu_0$: A Scalable 3D Interaction-Trace World Model

$\mu_0$ introduces a scalable world model by forecasting smooth 3D trajectories of key interaction points rather than predicting dense pixels or requiring specific action labels. The accompanying TraceExtract system enables training on diverse videos by automatically generating 3D supervision through keypoint selection and language caption association. This process pretrains $\mu_0$ using a vision-language backbone and a modular trace expert that represents motion via B-spline control points. Experiments confirm that $\mu_0$ excels at 2D and 3D trace prediction compared to existing baselines. Because it is action-free, the resulting model offers a transferable, compact representation for achieving state-of-the-art policy performance across different robot embodiments.

arXiv - cs.RO

FlowMo-WM: A World Model with Object Momentum and Hidden Ambient Drift

World models are used in robot learning to predict future states from visual observations and actions. Current action-conditioned models often neglect inertia and unmeasured external forces like currents or wind affecting real-world objects. FlowMo-WM is proposed as an end-to-end trainable model that infers object motion state alongside a context representing hidden ambient drift from image-action histories without explicit flow supervision. It factorizes the history into a short-history state for object dynamics and a longer-history context summarizing slow exogenous influences. Evaluations in simulated aquatic environments show FlowMo-WM improves long-horizon prediction accuracy by correctly accounting for these ambient drifts.

arXiv - cs.RO

Multi-Agent Embodied Autonomous Driving: From V2X Information Exchange to Shared World Models

Autonomous driving is evolving into multi-agent embodied systems that require sharing perception and coordinating actions under uncertainty. This survey reviews the transition using Shared World Models (SWMs), which are predictive representations across vehicles and infrastructure. It examines research covering V2X communication, collaborative perception, and cooperative planning from over 380 publications. Current evaluations heavily rely on simulations, while foundation-model coordination lacks proven real-time safety in open traffic. Future research must prioritize verifiable shared-state maintenance, robust intent alignment, and safe action despite deployment constraints like latency.

arXiv - cs.RO

ContactWorld: What Matters in Vision-Tactile World Models for Contact-Rich Manipulation

Contact-rich manipulation necessitates world models that can reason about complex contact dynamics from multi-modal sensing. The study introduces ContactWorld, benchmarking performance across 12 challenging tasks like insertion and screwing. Strongest planning performance was achieved by representations that are both spatially structured and temporally continuous. Incorporating point-cloud observations significantly boosted average success rates compared to wrist or front-view observations. Combining point-clouds with tactile force-field representations provided the best results, demonstrating the importance of representation structure and cross-modal compatibility for long-horizon planning.

arXiv - cs.RO

AnyGoal: Vision-Language Guided Multi-Agent Exploration for Training-Free Lifelong Navigation

AnyGoal is a training-free multi-robot system designed for lifelong navigation using a Vision-Language Model (VLM) as its core component. It coordinates agents through a shared 2D Gaussian Bayesian Value Map (BVM), which accumulates goal relevance evidence across subtasks without resetting. Frontier exploration is determined by combining the VLM's judgment with a Bayesian Upper Confidence Bound (UCB) calculated on the BVM. Agents distribute themselves across ranked frontiers using a greedy allocator incorporating spatial separation penalties. On GOAT-Bench, the dual-agent AnyGoal achieved state-of-the-art performance, significantly outperforming Modular GOAT under strict physical constraints.

arXiv - cs.RO

Guided Diffusion with Distilled Vision-Language Reliability for Aerial Navigation

Autonomous UAV navigation often suffers from error accumulation across separate perception, mapping, and planning stages. End-to-end generative models fail because they treat unreliable observations, like reflections or overexposure, as valid data. The presented work introduces a reliability-aware diffusion planner that conditions trajectory generation on both observation and a scene-level reliability heatmap. This heatmap is generated by distilling the open-vocabulary reasoning of a vision-language model within real-time constraints. Testing showed this method significantly improved safety and traversal reliability compared to previous state-of-the-art baselines, confirming the crucial role of the added reliability term.
