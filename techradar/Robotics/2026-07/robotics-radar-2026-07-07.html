GigaWorld-1: A Roadmap to Build World Models for Robot Policy Evaluation

Evaluating embodied robot policies is difficult due to slow, costly real-world rollouts, prompting interest in using world models as evaluators. This work introduces WMBench, a benchmark built from real-robot teleoperation data to allow controlled comparisons across various model and encoding types. Analysis of 7 video world models using WMBench showed evaluator quality relies heavily on long-horizon, action-faithful consistency rather than short-term visuals. Findings also indicated that pretraining benefits require balancing general knowledge with robot-specific controllability and that architectural choices significantly impact real-world alignment. Consequently, the authors developed and released \textit{GigaWorld-1}, a world model optimized specifically for policy evaluation in this domain.

EVA-Client: A Unified Data Collection, Inference, and Deployment Framework for Embodied Policies on Real Robots

EVA-Client is an open-source framework designed for deploying, collecting data from, and evaluating manipulation policies on actual robots. It unifies the policy iteration loop stages by sitting between the policy server and the physical hardware. The framework features a component-decoupled architecture, allowing modular additions of different robot backends or inference strategies. EVA-Client supports inspectable execution through Debug, Collect, and Eval workflows, ranging from simulation to real-time control. Furthermore, every evaluation automatically records data in a training-ready format, ensuring continuous feedback for subsequent policy refinement.

MorphQuad: Morphable Quadrotor for Superhuman Maneuverability, Manipulation, and Resiliency

MorphQuad is presented as a morphable quadrotor designed to achieve extreme maneuverability, manipulation, and resiliency for tasks like infrastructure maintenance. Achieving these capabilities requires vectoring maximum thrust in any direction, global stability control, and compact integration onto existing platforms. The system employs a hardware/control co-design where each of the four rotors uses independently articulated two-axis gimbals. Control mechanisms include globally-stable control and energy-optimal thrust allocation to manage inter-rotor interference. Testing confirms MorphQuad's ability to perform multi-revolution rotation, valve manipulation, and robust wind rejection from any source.

Metallic Ultrasound Waveguides as a Distributed Tactile Sensing Platform for Contact Localization, Force Estimation, and Material Class Discrimination

Metallic ultrasound waveguides show promise for advanced tactile sensing by interrogating contacts from one proximal transducer. Experiments using cylindrical indenters demonstrated that applied force during single point indentation is linearly correlated with the reflection-to-transmission coefficient ratio ($F = a \cdot R/T$). Furthermore, the reflected energy partition proved to be a load-independent measure, allowing material classification regardless of contact force. The methodology successfully recovered two separate contact forces from the waveguide signal using two indenters. These findings validate metallic waveguides as a robust platform for simultaneously localizing contacts, estimating forces, and discriminating materials.

TACO: TActile World Model as a Self-COrrector forScalable VLA Post-Training

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models struggle with contact-rich tasks due to unrecoverable failures undetectable by vision alone. Introducing TACO, a framework, addresses this limitation through tactile-aware world model-driven post-training. TACO operates via a Recognize-Imagine-Label loop using real robot rollouts for iterative improvement. It employs a progress-action model to identify failure states and a visuo-tactile generation model to imagine local corrective segments. Experiments demonstrate that TACO significantly improves success rates on real-world contact manipulation tasks by converting failures into imagined corrections.

CLEAR: Closed-Loop Reinforcement Learning at Scale for End-to-End Autonomous Driving

End-to-end autonomous driving (E2E-AD) using Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models faces performance gaps when moving from imitation learning to closed-loop planning due to distribution shift. CLEAR introduces a system that addresses this by enabling large-scale, closed-loop training through Reinforcement Learning for E2E-AD. The approach learns a novel residual waypoint policy that builds upon knowledge embedded in pretrained VLA policies. To handle the data hunger of vision-based RL, it utilizes a heterogeneous pipeline to maximize parallel simulation environments without resource conflicts. Evaluation demonstrates that CLEAR significantly outperforms existing methods on benchmarks like CARLA longest6 v2 and Bench2Drive using simple reward signals.

Differential Amplifier-Inspired AmpAttention for Multi-View Robotic Manipulation

Proposes AmpAttention, a novel mechanism drawing inspiration from differential amplifiers to mitigate attention drift in multi-view robotic manipulation. This approach aims to suppress noise and capture higher signal-to-noise ratio signals for robust perception. An integrated model called RVAF utilizes task-guided intra-view and inter-view AmpAttention across 18 RLBench tasks. Compared to existing methods, RVAF achieves optimal average success rates while significantly reducing training time by 33.3%. Further enhancing the system with SAM2's image encoder results in RVAF++, which demonstrated a 91% success rate on precise peg insertion tasks.

DREAMSTEER: Latent World Models Can Steer VLA Policies During Deployment Without Any Finetuning

Pretrained Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policies struggle with robustness when facing distribution shift during deployment. DREAMSTEER introduces a novel, finetuning-free framework to guide these policies in real-world scenarios. The method utilizes an action-conditioned latent world model to simulate potential outcomes from candidate actions and motion primitives. A language-conditioned value model is then employed to rank the imagined trajectories based on their predicted quality. Testing on four manipulation benchmarks with unseen objects showed DREAMSTEER significantly boosted task success rates (from 23.75% to 66.25%) compared to the base VLA policy.

arXiv - cs.RO

Continuous-Time Gaussian Belief Trees for Motion Planning

Presents a sampling-based motion planning approach for continuous-time stochastic systems facing process and measurement uncertainties while providing safety guarantees. It models robot dynamics using continuous-time linear stochastic differential equations, with measurements sampled discretely. An offline hybrid belief propagation model tracks belief evolution via continuous ODEs punctuated by Kalman filter updates. Safety is ensured through a novel belief-barrier-function checker that verifies safety over entire continuous trajectory segments. Integration with RRT and SST planners demonstrates high success rates and robust chance constraint enforcement, particularly in narrow passages where discrete methods fail.

arXiv - cs.RO

Longitudinal-Motion-Aware Lateral Control for Autonomous Vehicles: A Robust Nonlinear Control Framework

Presents a longitudinal-motion-aware robust nonlinear lateral control framework for autonomous vehicles operating in dynamic traffic. The framework develops a tracking error model incorporating varying longitudinal speed and acceleration, using feedback linearization to embed this motion into the control law. Two robust designs are proposed to handle parameter uncertainty: a Lyapunov redesign (LR) inspired by sliding mode control, and an incremental nonlinear dynamic inversion (INDI) method. Both methods are analyzed for stability and proven to ensure ultimate boundedness. Simulations and real-vehicle tests confirm enhanced tracking accuracy across diverse speeds, accelerations, and model uncertainties.
