A new debit card, the EAT Card, launched via an app aims to fight hunger by funding relief through merchant transaction fees. Funds are loaded via standard ACH transfers from a US bank account, and the card is issued by i3 Bank under Visa licensing. The initiative extends to a separate business card for organizations through a partnership with Crowded.

Perimenopause misinformation and treatment realities

Perimenopause is the often confusing period before menopause, characterized by fluctuating hormone levels. Information surrounding perimenopause is abundant but often inaccurate, leading to misinformation about treatment options. Hormone Replacement Therapy (HRT) may help some symptoms but has limitations in perimenopausal women and carries risks like abnormal bleeding. Marketing for supplements lacks strong evidence, and not all midlife symptoms are directly caused by hormonal changes. Therefore, seeking medical advice rather than relying solely on social media trends is recommended for symptom management.
