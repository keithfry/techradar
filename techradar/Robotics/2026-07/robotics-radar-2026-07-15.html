ROS Discourse General: Ros2_info — a terminal workspace lens for ROS 2 (TUI + optional local AI)

ros2_info is a fastfetch-style ROS 2 workstation dashboard designed as a full-screen TUI written in Rust for headless operation. It consolidates vital debugging information—including live nodes, topics, and build status—into a single view to reduce context switching from multiple terminals. The tool features an integrated PTY terminal and multi-tab editing with Neovim bindings. Additionally, it includes optional offline local AI assistance via Ollama for tasks like error explanation and autonomous code iteration, all implemented with diff-gating. It supports various ROS 2 distributions and offers advanced features such as a graph canvas and sandboxed experimentation mode.

Enabling 24-hour Agricultural Robotics: Unsupervised Day-to-Night Cross-Modal Image Translation for Nighttime Visual Navigation

Current agricultural robotics vision systems mainly function during the day, hindering 24-hour monitoring capabilities like nocturnal pest detection. To overcome the lack of nighttime datasets, researchers propose an unsupervised image translation framework converting daytime RGB images to near-infrared (NIR) nighttime versions without needing explicit supervision. This method utilizes a pre-trained CLIP model to maintain semantic consistency across the day-to-night conversion and includes a visibility mask for NIR limitations. Evaluation on the novel AgriNight dataset shows improved performance in downstream semantic segmentation tasks compared to existing baselines. The work supports real-time autonomous navigation testing with physical robots operating at night.

arXiv - cs.RO

Robust In-Hand Manipulation via Priors in Reinforcement Learning and Mechanical Design

Achieving robust in-hand manipulation without external sensing is difficult due to contact uncertainties and gravity disturbances. The research introduces two physics priors—a global grasp-quality prior and a local contact-geometry prior—to enhance learned rolling stability. The grasp-quality prior functions as a dense reward term, promoting better distributed contacts and wrench resistance. Meanwhile, the contact-geometry prior mechanically shapes the fingertip interface for task-aligned rolling and drift reduction. Evaluations on multifingered hands manipulating various objects show these priors significantly boost rotation efficiency, stability, and disturbance rejection during in-hand rolling tasks.

arXiv - cs.RO

GaitSpan: Growing Humanoid Locomotion from Walking to Running

GaitSpan is a novel framework designed to expand a basic pretrained walking policy to enable faster gaits like jogging and running for humanoids. It treats initial walking as a reusable seed skill that governs essential motor structures like balance and coordination. The expansion involves three mechanisms: rhythm generation using internal clocks, stride shaping based on inverted pendulum dynamics, and residual adaptation for fine motion details. This approach yields a single command-conditioned policy capable of spanning continuous speed ranges across different gaits. Furthermore, GaitSpan demonstrates zero-shot transferability to unseen terrains in simulation and the real world, outperforming multi-expert or imitation learning baselines.

A Behavioral State Vocabulary in Sony ERS-111 R-CODE

A corpus-level analysis examined behavior diagrams from Sony's R-CODE sample distribution for the ERS-111 AIBO. Instead of analyzing individual scripts, the study compared named states across the entire corpus. This revealed that diverse routines are built upon a compact embodied grammar. The core vocabulary revolves around initialization, sensing, iterative action, synchronization, and recovery. Consequently, this state-based abstraction is proposed as valuable for creating new, modular behavior routines on constrained robotic systems.

More than a Manipulator: Planning Propellant-Free Attitude Maneuvers for Free-Floating Spacecraft

Attitude control of spacecraft traditionally relies on thrusters or momentum exchange devices. This research explores utilizing robotic manipulator movements for propellant-free reorientation instead of treating them solely as disturbances. A trajectory optimization approach, incorporating joint and collision constraints, was developed and solved using an interior point solver. Demonstrations show that complex slew and detumble maneuvers are feasible across diverse spacecraft-manipulator systems. The study proves manipulators can function as primary or redundant attitude control actuators, benefiting in applications like in-space assembly.

Analysis of Mutual and Referential Human and Robot Gazes in a Collaborative Word Association Game

Robot gaze is crucial for coordinating human-robot dialogue, especially in task-focused settings beyond simple conversation. This study examined how a robot's gaze impacts human attention during a collaborative word association game with an LLM-driven NAO robot. Under conditions of mutual or referential robot gazing, participants' time to first fixation on proposed words was unaffected by the robot's gaze orientation. However, participants were observed directing their gaze more frequently toward the robot when confirmation requests were present in the dialogue. The findings suggest that the cognitively demanding nature of the task may overshadow the specific effects of the robot's referential gaze cues during collaborative work.

arXiv - cs.RO

DiffRadar: Differentiable Physics-Aware Radar SLAM with Gaussian Fields

DiffRadar is a new, real-time Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) system designed for radar sensing. Instead of using discrete scan matching on heatmaps, it models radar observations as a differentiable, physics-aware Gaussian field. By representing the scene with anisotropic Gaussian primitives, DiffRadar uses a differentiable radar forward model to optimize robot pose and scene structure directly from measurements in range-azimuth and Doppler-azimuth spaces. Tested on commodity FMCW hardware, it performs well on benchmarks like Radarize and custom stress tests. The system demonstrates significant reductions in trajectory error and improves map consistency while maintaining 70 FPS real-time performance.

Flatness-Preserving Residual Learning for Real-Time Tight Quadrotor Formation Flight

Turbulent aerodynamic interactions severely challenge quadrotors flying in close formations, risking collisions if unmodeled. A proposed physics-informed residual dynamics learning framework models these complex interactions while maintaining the system's differential flatness property. This preserved flatness enables a computationally efficient feedback linearization controller that uses feedforward compensation for disturbances. Hardware tests show this method reduces average tracking errors by 31% versus baseline methods. Furthermore, it matches state-of-the-art NMPC performance using significantly less computation and requires minimal training data.
