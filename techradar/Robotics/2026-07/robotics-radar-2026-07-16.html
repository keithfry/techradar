Autonomous UAV Route Planning for Coverage Maximization in Environmental Monitoring: A Systematic Literature Review

Environmental monitoring using UAVs necessitates route planning that maximizes coverage while respecting energy and operational limits. This paper presents preliminary results from a systematic literature review following the PRISMA 2020 framework, searching databases for studies from 2015 to 2026. The review focuses on path, coverage, and informative planning, analyzing algorithmic families and associated metrics. Initial findings show significant research emphasis on coverage-oriented approaches and multi-UAV coordination. However, the literature also indicates a scarcity of work addressing weather uncertainty or complex obstacles, suggesting a need for structured synthesis to advance realistic field applications.

arXiv - cs.RO

HRIBench: Benchmarking Interaction-Centric Human-Robot Collaboration

Existing vision-language-action (VLA) benchmarks fail to model complex human-robot collaboration structures like intent understanding and temporal synchronization. HRIBench introduces a diagnostic benchmark using structured scenario scripts that explicitly model agent roles and coordination constraints for collaborative tasks. The benchmark defines three interaction roles—Instructor, Collaborator, and Intruder—across 13 task types with over 650 evaluation episodes. It employs interpretable metrics measuring synchronization, responsiveness, protocol compliance, and safety beyond simple success rates. Evaluation using HRIBench reveals that current foundation robot policies possess limitations in temporal coordination and intent-aware behavior within collaborative settings.

arXiv - cs.RO

Power from Potential: A Survey of Electrostatic Actuators for Haptics

High-Voltage Electrostatic Actuators (HVEAs) are presented as a promising solution for haptic interfaces due to their soft, thin, silent, and energy-efficient nature compared to conventional actuators. The paper reviews four main classes of HVEAs: electrostatic switchable adhesives, dielectric elastomer actuators, soft electrohydraulic actuators, and electrokinetic pumps. For each type, it details the mechanism, bandwidth, force density, and scalability for providing haptic feedback. This cross-technology analysis identifies shared design limitations and strategies for enhancing ergonomics and fabrication. Ultimately, it outlines HVEAs' unique potential to advance practical, wearable haptic interaction systems.

arXiv - cs.RO

GPUSimBench: Towards Scalable and Reliable GPU-Accelerated Simulators in Embodied AI

Embodied AI heavily relies on massive parallel simulation using GPU-accelerated tools, but key trade-offs like efficiency, fidelity, and determinism are understudied at scale. The research introduces GPUSimBench to expose hidden limitations in current robotic simulators (e.g., Isaac Lab). This benchmark evaluates physical consistency using an inclined-plane task to compare simulation dynamics with reality. Furthermore, it measures parallel scalability by assessing throughput across increasing environments. Crucially, the work quantifies inherent non-determinism in GPU batch execution, showing variability even when conditions are identical.

arXiv - cs.RO

A Bayesian framework for the uncanny valley in humanoid robot design

Proposes a hierarchical Bayesian generative model to operationalize existing, non-algorithmic guidelines for designing humanoid robots within the context of the uncanny valley. The model represents human affinity as posterior-weighted negative category-conditional surprise, linking ambiguity and mismatch to increased surprise. It maps uncanny-valley mechanisms onto four measurable variables: deviation from predicted mean, cross-modal inconsistency, prediction uncertainty, and observational uncertainty. Simulations demonstrated that ambiguity and appearance-motion mismatches reduce affinity, while uncertainty significantly reshapes the valley's effects. Human-subject experiments confirmed this, showing that greater observational uncertainty mitigated familiarity decreases at intermediate likeness levels.

arXiv - cs.RO

A 3DGS-Driven Dynamic Viewpoint and Vibrotactile Framework for Subsea Teleoperation Validated via fNIRS

A novel teleoperation architecture addresses limitations in subsea ROV operation using a ROS-Unity framework to separate planning from avoidance. It employs a Dynamic Adaptive Viewpoint System (DAVS), utilizing 3D Gaussian Splatting, to provide occlusion-free exocentric views rather than standard 2D feeds. Sensory load is further managed by mapping obstacle clearance to intuitive haptic cues via a vibrotactile suit. Testing on 30 subjects revealed that the 3DGS-driven exocentric view excelled under high communication latency (0.5–1.0 s). Additionally, fNIRS data showed that proactive spatial context from DAVS mitigated cognitive overload associated with increased latency in traditional egocentric control.

arXiv - cs.RO

Worlds in One Demo: A Synthetic Data Engine for Learning Open-World Mobile Manipulation

To overcome the prohibitive cost of manual data collection for open-world mobile manipulation, a synthetic data engine called WANDA is introduced. WANDA reconstructs background Gaussian splats and robot-object interaction trajectories from single RGBD observations to create a foundational world substrate. It then reconfigures contact-rich segments using whole-body motion planning to generate vast numbers of diverse spatial configurations. To boost robustness and generalization, the method employs Corrective State Expansion and synthesizes data across varied 3D worlds derived from everyday photos. Experiments demonstrate that policies trained with WANDA achieve strong long-horizon, spatial, and cross-environment generalization, even supporting zero-shot deployment on different robot morphologies.

arXiv - cs.RO

Attitude Estimation Using Inertial and Barometric Measurements

Accurate attitude estimation is challenging for autonomous vehicles, especially without GNSS or during high acceleration when IMUs alone fail. This paper presents two barometer-aided architectures to enhance estimation by utilizing altitude data for vertical motion information. The first approach designs a deterministic Riccati observer cascaded with a complementary filter, ensuring almost-global asymptotic stability under uniform observability. Secondly, a novel nonlinear observer is proposed that integrates IMU inputs with barometer and magnetometer outputs within an SO(3)xR2 framework, guaranteeing local exponential stability. Validation using simulated and real data shows this method offers a practical and reliable sensing modality when conventional velocity measurements are unavailable.

arXiv - cs.RO

Parsimonious disturbance-aware minimum-time planning with parametric uncertainty

A minimum-lap-time planning framework incorporating robustness is presented for motorsport applications. This methodology extends prior work by managing both state disturbances and uncertainty in vehicle parameters like moment of inertia and drag coefficient. To maintain computational feasibility, a spatially selective, parsimonious strategy limits robust constraints to critical circuit segments. Validation was performed using an MPC as a virtual driver on a simulated FSAE car over 1000 runs with random disturbances. The resulting robust references demonstrated improved driveability, exhibiting fewer failures and better signal dispersion compared to nominal plans at a minor time cost.
