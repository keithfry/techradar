Adaptive Control of Motor-Position-Controlled Flexible Joint Robots with Uncertain Joint Stiffness

Accurate joint stiffness knowledge is crucial for model-based control of flexible joint robots, but precise stiffness models are often unavailable due to changing physical properties. This work proposes an adaptive control approach to address this by updating estimates of the uncertain, nonlinear torque-deflection relation of each joint. The method utilizes an implicit control law and a control-input-dependent regressor matrix to handle the uncertain joint stiffness. Robustness against errors from the motor position controller is also analyzed within the proposed framework. Experimental results confirm the effectiveness of this adaptive approach on flexible joints with nonlinear stiffness.

Semantic Audio-driven Understanding for Dynamic Humanoid Whole Body Control

A novel multi-modal orchestration framework is introduced for semantic audio-driven humanoid control, allowing robots to autonomously select and execute motion skills in real time. The system processes continuous audio streams into music or speech branches using techniques like audio fingerprinting for music and imitation learning for speech. Music input is mapped to motion policies via semantic embeddings for temporal alignment, while speech input is grounded into a library of imitation-learned skills. A unified interface schedules skill execution over a reinforcement learning control pipeline. Validation in simulation and on a Unitree G1 humanoid demonstrated robust sim-to-real transfer and consistent audio-conditioned policy selection.

MEMORA: Embodied Action Memory from Egocentric Videos for Reasoning and Planning

MEMORA formulates Embodied Action Memory (EAM) as a persistent memory state for robot planning by capturing embodied experience from egocentric videos. It implements a lifecycle including formation, consolidation, and retrieval across four typed stores: Environment, Entity, Activity, and Inferred Knowledge. Overall, editable and consolidated memory effectively provides remembered context for complex robot planning.

arXiv - cs.RO

Stochastic Filtering for Quorum Sensing in Robot Swarms under Anonymous Communication

Quorum Sensing in robot swarms requires effective communication, often using anonymous protocols to maintain scalability. Anonymous communication can lead to biased quorum estimates due to repeated messages from the same source being double-counted. A stochastic filtering protocol inspired by $k$-priority sampling ($\text{ANT}_k$) is introduced to enhance estimate stability compared to baseline methods. While a randomized variant improves accuracy, it introduces information inertia causing slower convergence. Actively filtering the message buffer with $\text{ANT}_k$ successfully reduces temporary errors and stabilizes estimates, though recovery from errors takes more time.

arXiv - cs.RO

DiMaS: Distribution Matching for Steering Vision-Language-Action Models

Flow-matching-based vision-language-action (VLA) models lack fine-grained behavioral control, which DiMaS addresses through a Distribution-Matching Steering strategy. This method transports between representation distributions instead of steering along fixed directions, effectively controlling behavior across two VLA systems. The research also investigates the generalizability of this steering strategy as tasks vary. Analysis reveals that classical linear steering is insufficient because behavioral features are linearly decodable but not linearly steerable in visuomotor settings. Ultimately, DiMaS proposes a distribution-matching approach to achieve superior control in vision-language-action models.

arXiv - cs.RO

ConFlow: Constraints-Guided Learning with Flow Matching for Motion Generation

ConFlow is a constraint-guided flow matching framework designed for motion generation in robotics. It incorporates constraint information directly into the training objective using differentiable barrier or cost functions. To enforce design specifications like smoothness and boundary conditions, it replaces the standard Gaussian source distribution with a conditional Gaussian Process. The method also utilizes infeasible demonstrations for negative supervision to improve constraint satisfaction without extra expert data. Experiments show ConFlow results in lower collision rates and higher trajectory quality compared to standard flow matching methods.
