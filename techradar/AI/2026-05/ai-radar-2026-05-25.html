BOHM: Zero-Cost Hierarchical Attribution for Compound AI Systems

Compound AI systems route tasks through hierarchies of specialized components, making traditional attribution methods impractical for certain parts of the system. BOHM introduces a zero-cost hierarchical attribution method that extracts an attribution tree directly from the system's routing weights, providing multi-resolution attribution at every level simultaneously. Unlike SHAP, BOHM does not require access to component internals and provides accurate attribution even when most coalitions are un-evaluable. BOHM converges with SHAP when deployed optimally, but satisfies different questions and has its own limitations.

The Readout Shortcut: Positional Number Copying Dominates Arithmetic CoT Readout in Small Language Models

In three 1-3B instruction-tuned LMs on GSM8K, chain-of-thought (CoT) prompting was found to rely heavily on a positional shortcut: the model copies the last number in the sequence before the answer delimiter with high accuracy, even when intermediate reasoning is incorrect.
