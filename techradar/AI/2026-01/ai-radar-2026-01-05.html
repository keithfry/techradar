1. DeepSeek Researchers Apply a 1967 Matrix Normalization Algorithm to Fix Instability in Hyper Connections

RSS • 2026-01-04T03:03:22.000Z

DeepSeek researchers addressed training instability in large language models caused by hyper connections, which extend residual streams but suffer from explosive signal amplification. They developed Manifold Constrained Hyper Connections (mHC), applying the 1967 Sinkhorn Knopp algorithm to constrain residual mixing matrices onto the manifold of doubly stochastic matrices, thereby controlling norm growth and stabilizing training. This approach significantly reduces worst-case amplification from ~3000 to ~1.6, improves downstream benchmark performance (e.g., +2.1% BBH accuracy on a 27B model), and incurs only a modest 6.7% training time overhead. mHC introduces a new scaling dimension for LLM architecture by explicitly structuring and constraining residual pathways, promising more stable and efficient agentic AI and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) model training.
