2. AI Interview Series #4: Explain KV Caching

RSS • 2025-12-21T09:23:44.000Z

The article explains KV caching as a critical optimization for autoregressive large language models (LLMs) in production, addressing the inefficiency where token generation slows down as sequence length grows due to repeated recomputation of attention over all prior tokens. KV caching stores and reuses key and value tensors from previous tokens, reducing redundant attention calculations and maintaining nearly linear inference time. Benchmark results show enabling KV caching can speed up generation by nearly 5×, making it essential for scalable, efficient LLM deployment. This is highly relevant for topics like LLM optimization, agentic AI, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and frameworks such as LangChain where inference latency impacts real-time applications.
