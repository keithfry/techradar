SASGeo: Stability-Aware Semantic Map Localization for GNSS-Denied UAVs -- A Framework and Synthetic Proof of Concept

Presents \sas, a framework for precise semantic map localization when GNSS signals are unavailable to UAVs. This method enhances standard visual-inertial odometry by using persistent environmental structures like roads and buildings for absolute fixes. It integrates multiple cues including semantic raster alignment, relational graph evidence, and feature stability while actively rejecting ambiguous position estimates. Testing involved 220 randomized trials under various degradations like occlusion and scale changes. Results showed that spatial semantic matching variants achieved high recall rates (94.5-95.5%), suggesting the strength of structured geometric data in disambiguation.

arXiv - cs.RO

Physics-Guided Biomechanical Gait Adaptation for Humanoid Locomotion on Extreme Sloped Terrains

Humanoid locomotion control on steep slopes is difficult due to persistent gravitational bias, often causing policies to default to slow, crouched gaits. Researchers propose HumoSlope, a novel two-stage framework for robust movement across various sloped terrains. Stage I establishes balance by using a slope-adaptive Zero Moment Point (ZMP) regularizer on the local inclined support plane. Stage II introduces the Biomechanical Slope Gait Adapter (BSGA), which uses terrain descriptors to modulate rewards, encouraging appropriate propulsion and braking motions. Testing confirms this physics-guided approach allows for continuous, blind traversal of outdoor grass slopes up to $32.1^\circ$.

arXiv - cs.RO

Shift & Drift: A Zero-Shot Benchmark for Generalizable and Robust Autonomous Driving Motion Planning

Presents Shift & Drift, a dual-track benchmark to rigorously test autonomous driving motion planners against distribution shifts. The Semantic Shift Track adapts DeepScenario Open 3D data for zero-shot evaluation across German and San Francisco scenarios. Concurrently, the State-Distribution Drift Track injects stochastic perturbations to measure planner robustness to execution errors. Evaluation reveals that imitation learning struggles with semantic shift and drift from actuation noise. Reinforcement learning approaches demonstrated better performance, suggesting a trade-off between imitation fidelity and closed-loop resilience for reliable deployment.

arXiv - cs.RO

STEMbot: A Compliant Robot for Under-Canopy Plant Navigation

STEMbot is a miniature climbing robot designed to autonomously navigate under plant canopies for early pest detection in organic agriculture. It overcomes limitations of existing systems by integrating a geometric PIN-SLAM pipeline with a semantic OcTree for robust localization and mapping while climbing. Motion planning utilizes a manifold-constrained A* planner and ray-tracing goal specification, enabling branch-aware traversal. Hardware testing demonstrated reliable traversal on stems ranging from 7 to 33mm across four different plant specimens. Quantitative evaluations confirmed the system achieves high-fidelity geometric reconstructions with an average Chamfer distance under 1cm.

arXiv - cs.RO

Time-to-Collision Based Dynamic Obstacle Avoidance Using Pretrained Vision Models for Robots in Unstructured Environments

A novel, data-efficient method achieves dynamic obstacle avoidance using real-world video data without needing large datasets or simulation training. It utilizes a pre-trained monocular depth model (UniDepth) to generate dense depth maps from RGB video alone. Obstacle tracking is performed by projecting SuperPoint/SuperGlue keypoints into 3D space and calculating time-to-collision (TTC). The system then selects an evasive 2D motion primitive based on the minimum-TTC keypoint's closest point of approach. Tested on M3ED data, it shows high performance, detecting near collisions for most tested obstacles while avoiding model training entirely.

arXiv - cs.RO

Monocular Vision Based Control Framework for Grasping

Develops a unified grasping framework utilizing only monocular RGB vision to handle diverse objects, including soft and rigid materials. The system integrates open-vocabulary detection, segmentation, and real-time point tracking to estimate object motion and geometry. A unique language-based stiffness model infers an object's compliance from its semantic description to select the initial grasp strategy. For deformable items, grasp adaptation uses a keypoint dissimilarity measure as a visual proxy for deformation. Validation on varied objects like lettuce and plastic bottles proves stable grasping using only visual feedback with a position-controlled gripper.

arXiv - cs.RO

Soft Robotic Exogloves for Dexterous Mobility -- Towards Personalized Rehabilitation

Pneumatically-actuated soft robotic exogloves were designed and tested to improve hand rehabilitation and dexterity by achieving personalized fits. The research utilized topological scans for precise tailoring of the glove to an individual's hand anatomy, which was fabricated via silicone mold casting. Finite element analysis simulated human-robot interaction forces using a personalized biomechanical finger model. Pneumatic pressure control experiments demonstrated accurate, targeted mobility at the MCP and PIP joints with intrinsic stiffness. These findings establish a foundation for optimizing exoglove design to aid in dexterous manipulation and neuromuscular rehabilitation.

D-CLIPSE: Distributed Consensus-based Localization with Passive Listening on Shared State Exchange

Accurate and consistent multi-robot localization is crucial for planning and control tasks. Centralized filtering methods offer optimal fusion but are often impractical due to system constraints. This paper introduces D-CLIPSE, a distributed consensus-based filtering framework addressing these limitations. The method achieves efficiency by sharing preintegrated odometry and shared states among communicating robots. Validation in simulation and experiments demonstrated that the proposed approach nears centralized performance regarding both accuracy and consistency compared to current best practices.
