1. A New Google AI Research Proposes Deep-Thinking Ratio to Improve LLM Accuracy While Cutting Total Inference Costs by Half

RSS • 2026-02-22T04:53:19.000Z

New research from Google and the University of Virginia challenges the common practice of increasing Chain-of-Thought length to improve LLM accuracy, showing that longer outputs can reduce precision due to "overthinking." They introduce the Deep-Thinking Ratio (DTR), which measures how much processing happens in deeper transformer layers for each token, correlating strongly with model accuracy (r = 0.683). Leveraging DTR, their Think@n approach efficiently halts low-potential candidates early in inference, achieving 94.7% accuracy on a math benchmark while cutting total token generation—and thus costs—by nearly 50%. This method promises significant inference cost savings and accuracy gains for LLM deployments, impacting agentic AI and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) in frameworks like LangChain and LangGraph.
