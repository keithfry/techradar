1. In software, the code documents the app. In AI, the traces do.

RSS • 2026-01-10T17:39:27.000Z

This article highlights a fundamental shift in software engineering driven by AI agents and large language models (LLMs): unlike traditional software where the codebase is the source of truth for decision logic, AI agents embed their logic within runtime model behavior, making code just scaffolding. Consequently, traces—detailed logs of the agent’s reasoning steps, tool calls, and outcomes—become the new documentation, essential for debugging, testing, monitoring, optimizing, and collaboration. For teams building agentic AI with techniques like Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), PEFT, or LangChain, investing in robust trace observability platforms is critical to understand and improve model-driven decisions, maintain quality, and integrate product analytics. Without such trace-centric tooling, engineering efforts risk being blind to the true behavior of AI-powered systems.
