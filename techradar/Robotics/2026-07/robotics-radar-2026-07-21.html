Building robots for unpredictable, infrastructure-free environments

Robots that can operate effectively in unpredictable, infrastructure-free environments face significant challenges, including reliability, environmental variability, and the need for real-world deployment to accumulate data. Most robotics companies fail because they optimize for controlled lab conditions rather than real-world scenarios. Burro Robotics, which operates in outdoor agricultural settings, has learned about tolerance and environmental variability through hands-on experience, and believes that a fundamentally different engineering achievement is needed to perform reliably across all conditions. Infrastructure-free localization and perception are key areas of research investment that will unlock the next phase of autonomous robotics for industrial environments.

ROS Discourse General: PyZeROS: a Python-first alternative to `rclpy` over Zenoh

PyZeROS is an experimental Python-first alternative to `rclpy` for communicating with ROS 2, offering minimal dependencies, no ROS installation required, and asyncio executor. It can install through pip and communicates with ROS 2 through Zenoh, interoperating with standard ROS 2 nodes using rmw_zenoh_cpp. PyZeROS is designed for asyncio and asynchronous Python, providing custom ROS messages defined directly with Python classes.

ROS Discourse General: OnSLAM: Run LiDAR-inertial mapping on ROS1 bags directly from Windows

OnSLAM is an open-source Windows application that runs LiDAR-inertial odometry and mapping on ROS1 bag files. It automatically detects compatible sensors, visualizes the trajectory and point-cloud map in a browser, and exports PLY and PCD files for easy inspection of datasets. The project aims to simplify the process of inspecting datasets without requiring a complete Linux and ROS environment setup. Users are encouraged to test the application with different sensors and provide feedback, particularly on Livox, Ouster, Velodyne, or Hesai data.

Real-Time sEMG-Based Telecontrol of an Assistive Robotic Arm Using a 1D Convolutional Neural Network

A real-time sEMG-based telecontrol system for an assistive robotic arm using a 1D convolutional neural network (CNN) achieves high classification performance, with a test accuracy above 90%, and stable real-time behavior. The system uses four-channel sEMG acquisition, signal preprocessing, segmentation into sliding windows, and classification. It is implemented in simulation and on a real robotic platform, producing coherent and smooth movements. The system demonstrates the feasibility of sEMG-based telecontrol for assistive robotics.

Linear Stability Analysis of an INDI Pitch-Rate Controller under Model Mismatch for a Tilt-Rotor VTOL UAV

A closed-form fifth-order transfer function is derived for the full controller-estimator-actuator-plant interconnection, and stability is characterized through the Routh-Hurwitz criterion over a parameterized linear model. Two representative three-parameter sweeps produce interpretable stability regions, and two uncertainty-aware tuning procedures are proposed: robustness-oriented design that maximizes gain margin and phase margin, and performance-oriented design that maximizes worst-case closed-loop bandwidth subject to margin constraints.

arXiv - cs.RO

Certifiable Safe Model-Based Reinforcement Learning with Control-Affine Dynamics Approximation

This paper proposes a safe reinforcement learning framework that learns control-affine dynamics with a certifiable data-driven safe policy using control barrier functions (CBF). It uses Control-Affine Random Fourier Features (ARFF) to model robot dynamics in a control-affine form and adaptive conformal prediction (ACP) to quantify the uncertainty in the safety constraint. The proposed framework demonstrates effectiveness on simulation results for the cartpole and 3D quadrotor platforms.

arXiv - cs.RO

Foresight Residual RL for Long-Horizon Robot Manipulation with Vision-Language-Action Models

Foresight Residual RL for Long-Horizon Robot Manipulation with Vision-Language-Action Models optimizes handoff quality by augmenting subtask rewards with an offline-estimated foresight value, the probability of future subtask success conditioned on the terminal state of the current subtask. The method achieves 85.6% full-task success in a three-phase wrench-based nut-tightening assembly task, outperforming standard subtask residual RL and VLA baselines, while leaving per-subtask success unchanged.

arXiv - cs.RO

Differentiable Reinforcement Learning for Path Tracking by an Agile Fish-Like Robot

Prashanth Chivkula and his co-authors developed a simulation platform that approximates the motion of a fish-like robot with computational efficiency. They applied PID control, where gains were learned using back propagation through time and training on a curriculum, to perform motion control and path tracking by the robot. The policy learned in the simulation was then applied on the physical platform, demonstrating an excellent match for agile fish-like robots.
