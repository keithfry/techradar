AgenticFocus: Object-Preserving Mixed Reality Synthesis from Human FPV Video for Dexterous Humanoid Learning

AgenticFocus is a Mixed Reality synthesis pipeline designed to transform standard first-person human videos into robot-trainable demonstrations for humanoid learning. It addresses limitations in existing pipelines by restoring occluded object geometry and reconstructing detailed hand motion from challenging video inputs. The method achieves this through camera-relative alignment and layered compositing techniques, enabling accurate retargeting to a humanoid body model. This process generates a dataset pairing focused visual observations with synchronized robot actions and states. Experimental results show AgenticFocus yields lower trajectory error and smoother wrist motion compared to existing cross-embodiment baselines.

arXiv - cs.RO

FlowDAgger: Human-in-the-Loop Adaptation of Generative Robot Policies in Latent Space

Generative robot policies struggle with novel scenarios outside their initial training distribution, making safe real-world adaptation difficult. FlowDAgger offers an efficient method to adapt these frozen policies using minimal human demonstrations directly within the latent space. It achieves this by inverting expert actions into corresponding noise inputs via reverse-time integration and local refinement. This resulting inverted noise trains a lightweight policy that guides the base model during deployment for rapid skill acquisition. Evaluations show FlowDAgger surpasses supervised fine-tuning and latent-space RL baselines while maintaining existing capabilities on separate tasks, providing practical adaptation for foundation models.

arXiv - cs.RO

SplatCtrl: Perception-Action Coupling via Gaussian Scene Representations and Reactive Robot Control

SplatCtrl is a novel framework designed for collision-free robotic arm control in unstructured and changing environments. It utilizes 3D Gaussian Splatting (3D-GS) combined with a hybrid voxel-based filtering and dynamic Gaussian relocation strategy for efficient real-time scene reconstruction from RGB-D data. The method develops continuous signed distance functions from isotropic Gaussians, offering stable collision probability estimates. These metrics integrate into control barrier functions to ensure safe, reactive motion planning. Testing in simulation, physical robots, and shared workspaces confirms its success in integrating perception and action within dynamic settings.

arXiv - cs.RO

Dec-MARVEL: Decentralized Multi-Agent Exploration without Communication under Budget Constraints

Dec-MARVEL is a decentralized framework designed for multi-UAV exploration under constraints such as unreliable communication and finite travel budgets. It enables coordination without explicit communication by utilizing incidental observations, treating any teammate visible in the field of view as a signal. The system employs a graph-attention actor that fuses local frontier geometry, observed teammate motion, and budget status to select safe waypoints. Training involves phase-conditioned critics and a mixture-based budget curriculum. Evaluations across various team sizes and budgets show Dec-MARVEL achieving superior exploration rates compared to baselines, with successful physical deployment demonstrated in real-world tests.

arXiv - cs.RO

Residual Physics-Informed Neural Networks for High-Fidelity BLDC Motor Modeling

Accurate dynamics modeling of Brushless DC (BLDC) motors is crucial for high-performance robotic joint control. A Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) utilizing a deep residual backbone was developed to create a continuous-time surrogate model of the full six-state BLDC motor dynamics. Given inputs like voltages and excitation parameters, the network predicts all state variables—rotor angle, angular velocity, currents, and temperature—while enforcing physical laws via a combined loss function. The training process employs curriculum scheduling for stability, finishing quickly on standard CPU hardware. Post-training inference achieves very low latencies (0.1–22 $\mu$s), demonstrating up to 118x speedup over conventional ODE solvers for real-time control.

arXiv - cs.RO

BeyondSight: Object Permanence for End-to-End Autonomous Driving

Autonomous driving systems struggle when key actors are temporarily hidden due to occlusions, causing standard models to lose track of these agents. BeyondSight is a novel framework addressing this by decoupling actor existence from immediate observability using persistent hypotheses. It temporally propagates and updates these hypotheses with available evidence, allowing joint perception, prediction, and planning for unobservable actors. To support rigorous evaluation, the authors introduced nuScenes-Permanence, an extension providing supervision for hidden agents. Experiments confirm that BeyondSight improves reasoning under occlusion by boosting detection performance and reducing planning errors compared to existing methods.

TactiDex: A Real-World Tactile-Guided Benchmark for Human-Like Dexterous Manipulation

Human-Object Interaction requires tactile feedback to achieve realistic dexterity, but current methods often neglect physical contact during robot imitation. Introducing TactiDex, the authors provide a new, real-world benchmark dataset linking whole-hand tactile signals with kinematic and object states. This framework enables moving dexterous tasks beyond simple movement mimicry toward accurate contact-level behavior. A proposed method, TactiSkill, utilizes a novel tri-component tactile reward to guide robotic execution using human demonstrations. Experiments confirm that TactiSkill outperforms others in both manipulation success and physical realism for single and bimanual tasks.

GenVid2Robot: From Video Generation to Robot Manipulation via Rigid-Geometric Consistency

Generated videos can guide robot manipulation but often lack physically actionable metrics like precise geometry or kinematic feasibility. The proposed GenVid2Robot framework converts these video motions into executable trajectories by enforcing rigid-geometric consistency. It samples semantic anchors from the initial frame and verifies if subsequent 2D motion is explained by the first frame's RGB-D geometry under an $SE(3)$ model. This process treats videos as uncertain hypotheses, transferring only geometrically consistent relative motion to grasp planning. By integrating this with mask-constrained grasping and a depth-compensation module, GenVid2Robot generates reliable execution trajectories for real-world robotics.
