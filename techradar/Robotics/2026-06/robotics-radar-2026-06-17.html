ALS Patient Speaks Independently Using Brain Implant Technology

Casey Harrell, who has ALS and is paralyzed, uses electrodes implanted in his brain for communication. He was fitted with the device following an operation in July 2023 after being diagnosed with ALS. The system records activity from the speech motor cortex, translating neural signals into discernible phonemes and words. Since initial use in 2023, Harrell has gained substantial independence, utilizing the BCI for communication, web browsing, and working. He has reportedly used the device over 3,800 hours at home without researchers present, establishing him as a "power user" of speech BCIs.

ROS Discourse General: ROS2 on STM32 serial to SBC

Developing a ROS 2 robot requires connecting an STM32 microcontroller running Zenoh-native Zephyr RTOS to an SBC utilizing rmw_zenoh on a Zenoh router. The author is evaluating three communication options: micro-ROS, Zenoh-Pico with rmw_zenoh_pico, and Pico-ROS. Concerns exist regarding the suitability of micro-ROS given existing Zenoh usage, the portability of Zenoh-Pico to STM32/Zephyr, and the lack of documentation for Pico-ROS on this hardware combination. Ultimately, the author seeks practical experience or alternative architectural suggestions for integrating these systems.

Extracting Semantics: LLM-Guided Automatic Population of Robot Ontology from URDF

Automatic generation of populated robot ontologies from Unified Robot Description Format (URDF) models is presented to address the bottleneck of manual ontology construction. Because URDF files lack inherent semantics beyond structure, Large Language Models (LLMs) are utilized to infer meaningful relationships by prompting them with established ontological concepts. The proposed pipeline enhances reliability through majority voting across multiple LLM queries and incorporates syntactic and schema-level validation. This method effectively converts low-level kinematic descriptions into structured, semantically rich knowledge representations. Initial evaluations show the approach successfully bridges the gap needed for explainable reasoning in human-robot interaction.

HRDX: A Large-Scale Vector HD-Map Dataset

Researchers introduce HRDX, a new, large-scale vector HD-map dataset spanning approximately 40 hours of driving across 1,400 km. The data is captured using six synchronized cameras, a 128-beam LiDAR, centimeter-level RTK GNSS/IMU, and aligned aerial orthoimagery. HRDX features annotations for 10 vector map classes and over 20 semantic and topological attributes. Evaluation demonstrates that the dataset's large scale improves online vector-map construction. Furthermore, incorporating aerial imagery provides a structural prior that enhances geometric map quality across various training setups.

ParkingTransformer: LLM-Enhanced End-to-End Trajectory Planning for Autonomous Parking

ParkingTransformer is a novel framework designed for end-to-end autonomous parking by integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) with multi-view perception. It addresses limitations in existing methods by using LLM features and trajectory queries directly from sensor data, bypassing dense Bird's-View representations. To enhance spatial reasoning, the method incorporates 3D positional encoding, and it uses a fixed-window streaming mechanism for efficient historical processing. A coarse-to-fine decoding strategy is employed to refine the generated planning trajectories progressively. Experiments on CARLA and real platforms showed promising results, achieving an 88.70% average success rate in the field.

ACE-Ego-0: Unifying Egocentric Human and Robotic Data for VLA Pretraining

ACE-EGO-0 is a novel framework designed to unify Vision-Language-Action (VLA) pretraining by integrating heterogeneous human and robotic data. It addresses the difficulty of joint training due to disparities in action spaces and embodiment structures. A key component converts large amounts of raw egocentric human videos into robot-compatible pseudo-action trajectories via a scalable pipeline. This framework utilizes a unified camera-space action representation and employs a reliability-aware objective, incorporating a human auxiliary loss for noisy supervision. Evaluations show that using this combined data significantly boosts performance on benchmarks like RoboCasa GR1 TableTop, achieving state-of-the-art results.

Contrastive Action-Image Pre-training for Visuomotor Control

Existing vision encoders for robotics suffer from data scarcity, lacking the paired vision and action data needed for visuomotor control. To address this, authors propose CAIP (Contrastive Action-Image Pre-training), which uses human hand poses from large egocentric videos as a proxy for robot end-effector actions. This method extracts 3D hand keypoints and learns a unified action-image representation using a contrastive objective. Training on extensive human video alongside limited robotic data, CAIP outperforms state-of-the-art encoders like DINOv2 and SigLIP. The technique achieves over 30% performance gains on difficult real-world dexterous manipulation tasks, demonstrating scalability for physical interaction representations.

VISTA: Scale-Aware Visual Navigation via Action History Conditioning

VISTA improves Vision Navigation Foundation Models by conditioning predictions on normalized action histories to mitigate physical inaccuracies arising from action normalization. Integrating a DINOv3 encoder enhances representation learning, allowing the model to capture spatial and geometric details even in visually repetitive settings. This approach addresses the critical vulnerability of current models when translating normalized actions to real-world robotics. The resulting framework generalizes robustly to out-of-distribution environments. Testing demonstrated 100% goal prediction accuracy and an average of 95% checkpoints crossed across unseen outdoor, forest, and office settings.

arXiv - cs.RO

Transformer-Based Warm-Starting for Feasible and Optimal Terminal Approach to Tumbling Objects with Space Manipulators

Real-time trajectory generation for robotic servicing near tumbling targets is difficult due to coupled nonlinear dynamics and safety constraints. A proposed framework addresses this by decomposing the problem into translational and attitude/torque allocation stages. It applies a causal transformer warm-start specifically to the computationally intensive, coupled attitude-manipulator torque stage. Testing showed that this learned warm-start reduced sequential convex programming (SCP) iterations by up to 28% and runtime by 23% for cost-optimal approaches. Furthermore, when used for nonconvex feasibility projection, it nearly halved the runtime while maintaining superior trajectory robustness compared to heuristic initialization.

Contactless Respiratory Monitoring on Heterogeneous Mobile Robots: A Multimodal Edge-Computing Framework

Contactless respiratory rate (RR) monitoring is vital for safe remote triage in emergencies but faces challenges from varied illumination and sensor limitations. This paper introduces a modality-adaptive framework utilizing multiple sensors—RGB, thermal, NIR, and low-light cameras—on edge-computing mobile robots. The system incorporates brightness adaptation, keypoint guidance for robust region extraction, and an SQI filter for accurate estimation. Evaluation across diverse robotic platforms confirmed the framework's generalizability without platform-specific tuning. Results defined operational ranges, noting RGB offers the broadest coverage, while specific modalities are suited for different distance and lighting conditions.
