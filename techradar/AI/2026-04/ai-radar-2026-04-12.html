Researchers from MIT, NVIDIA, and Zhejiang University Propose TriAttention: A KV Cache Compression Method That Matches Full Attention at 2.5× Higher Throughput

Long-chain reasoning poses significant computational demands for large language models, especially when generating thousands of tokens. These generated tokens must be stored in the Key-Value (KV) cache, which consumes considerable resources. Researchers from MIT, NVIDIA, and Zhejiang University developed TriAttention as a method to compress this KV cache. TriAttention is specifically designed to maintain accuracy comparable to full attention mechanisms. Furthermore, this compression technique achieves a notable 2.5× increase in throughput.
