Latest Research Papers

New arXiv preprints from March 19, 2026 (cs.AI and cs.LG sections). Ten additional stat.ML papers were also indexed — visit arxiv.org/rss/stat.ML for the full list.

Computer Science — Artificial Intelligence (cs.AI)

Generative AI-assisted Participatory Modeling in Socio-Environmental Planning under Deep Uncertainty
arXiv cs.AI
Uses LLMs to assist stakeholder participatory modeling for complex planning problems under deep uncertainty. Demonstrates how generative AI can translate natural-language stakeholder descriptions into quantitative models for collaborative decision-making.
Transformers are Bayesian Networks
arXiv cs.AI
Proves in five ways that every sigmoid transformer implements weighted loopy belief propagation on its implicit factor graph. This theoretical result provides new understanding of how transformers perform probabilistic inference and why they generalize.
Cascade-Aware Multi-Agent Routing: Spatio-Temporal Sidecars and Geometry-Switching
arXiv cs.AI
Introduces geometry-aware scheduling for symbolic graph networks (AI agent systems connected by delegation edges). Unlike geometry-blind schedulers, this approach models how failures propagate differently in tree-like versus cyclic agent topologies.
How Clued up are LLMs? Evaluating Multi-Step Deductive Reasoning in a Text-Based Game Environment
arXiv cs.AI
Implements a multi-agent text version of the Clue board game as a testbed for evaluating multi-step deductive reasoning, with six agents drawn from GPT-4o-mini and Gemini-2.5-Flash. Investigates whether fine-tuning on structured logic puzzles transfers to improved in-game performance.
AI Scientist via Synthetic Task Scaling
arXiv cs.AI
Presents a principled framework for training agentic systems that can perform ML research autonomously via synthetic task scaling. Addresses the gap that current LLMs often generate plausible-looking but ineffective research ideas.
Draft-and-Prune: Improving the Reliability of Auto-formalization for Logical Reasoning
arXiv cs.AI
Addresses brittleness in auto-formalization pipelines where programs may fail to execute or encode incorrect semantics. The draft-and-prune approach reduces both syntactic and semantic errors in translating natural language to formal logical representations.
Graph-Native Cognitive Memory for AI Agents: Formal Belief Revision Semantics for Versioned Memory Architectures
arXiv cs.AI
Introduces Kumiho, a graph-native cognitive memory architecture grounded in formal belief revision semantics. Provides structural primitives including immutable revisions and mutable tag pointers to support long-term agent memory management.
Contrastive Reasoning Alignment: Reinforcement Learning from Hidden Representations (CRAFT)
arXiv cs.AI
A red-teaming alignment framework that uses a model's hidden representations to improve robustness against jailbreak attacks. Unlike defenses that operate at the output level, CRAFT explicitly optimizes safety-aware reasoning traces.
InfoDensity: Rewarding Information-Dense Traces for Efficient Reasoning
arXiv cs.AI
An RL approach that rewards information-dense intermediate reasoning traces to curb LLM verbosity. Addresses the gap in existing approaches that optimize final response length while neglecting the quality of intermediate reasoning steps.
Physics-informed offline reinforcement learning eliminates catastrophic fuel waste in maritime routing (PIER)
arXiv cs.AI
PIER is an offline RL framework learning fuel-efficient, safety-aware routing policies from physics-calibrated environments. Targets maritime shipping, which accounts for approximately 3% of global greenhouse gas emissions.

Computer Science — Machine Learning (cs.LG)

A foundation model for electrodermal activity data
arXiv cs.LG
Addresses the lack of large-scale curated datasets for EDA (electrodermal activity) modeling with a new foundation model for sympathetic nervous system physiological signals. Opens doors for improved health and wellness applications using wearable sensor data.
Federated Multi Agent Deep Learning and Neural Networks for Advanced Distributed Sensing in Wireless Networks
arXiv cs.LG
Presents MADL as a unifying framework for decision-making and inference in 5G-Advanced and 6G wireless systems where sensing, communication, and computing are tightly coupled. Enables intelligent distributed sensing without centralized control.
Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning for Dynamic Pricing: Balancing Profitability, Stability and Fairness
arXiv cs.LG
Systematic empirical evaluation of MAPPO and MADDPG for dynamic price optimization in simulated competitive retail markets. Addresses tradeoffs between profitability, market stability, and pricing fairness using a multi-agent framework.
From Language to Action in Arabic: Reliable Structured Tool Calling via Data-Centric Fine-Tuning
arXiv cs.LG
Introduces AISA-AR-FunctionCall, a production-oriented Arabic function-calling framework built on a 270M-parameter FunctionGemma base. Enables Arabic-language LLMs to reliably invoke structured tools and APIs in agentic pipelines.
What on Earth is AlphaEarth? Hierarchical structure and functional interpretability for global land cover
arXiv cs.LG
Interpretability study of Google AlphaEarth Foundation (GAEF) geospatial embeddings revealing whether hierarchical structure maps meaningfully to global land cover categories. Extends prior work on continuous environmental variable analysis to categorical structure.
HoloByte: Continuous Hyperspherical Distillation for Tokenizer-Free Modeling
arXiv cs.LG
Proposes replacing discrete subword tokenization with continuous hyperspherical representations to eliminate artificial morphological boundaries and vocabulary dependence. Resolves discontinuities in the optimization landscape caused by tokenization quantization.
MHPO: Modulated Hazard-aware Policy Optimization for Stable Reinforcement Learning
arXiv cs.LG
Addresses non-differentiable boundaries and vanishing gradient regions in GRPO-based RL frameworks with hazard-aware importance ratio regulation. Maintains gradient fidelity while improving training stability in complex RL tasks.
Integrating Explainable Machine Learning and Mixed-Integer Optimization for Personalized Sleep Quality Intervention
arXiv cs.LG
Bridges ML sleep quality predictions to actionable personalized interventions through mixed-integer optimization. Moves beyond predictive risk identification to provide concrete, implementable lifestyle recommendations.
Minimum-Action Learning: Energy-Constrained Symbolic Model Selection for Physical Law Identification from Noisy Data
arXiv cs.LG
Introduces MAL, which selects symbolic force laws from a library by minimizing a Triple-Action functional combining trajectory reconstruction, architectural sparsity, and energy conservation. Designed for discovering governing equations from noisy observational data.
Formal verification of tree-based machine learning models for lateral spreading
arXiv cs.LG
Applies formal verification to tree-based ML models for geotechnical hazard (lateral spreading) prediction, addressing physically inconsistent relationships learned from sparse or biased data. Goes beyond SHAP/LIME explainability to provide correctness guarantees.
