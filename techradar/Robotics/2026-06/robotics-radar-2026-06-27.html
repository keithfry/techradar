Australian rescue team uses AI-powered drone to find lost hikers – video

Two men in their 20s were located using an artificial intelligence-powered drone that employed thermal imaging. The hikers had deviated from the established walking track in Kosciuszko national park, New South Wales. They were found approximately half a kilometre off the main trail on Tuesday. This incident marked the first deployment of the FRNSW drone's AI detection system for missing person rescues. Authorities successfully located the individuals within five hours of them going missing.

MIT News - Robotics

LLMs help robots understand vague instructions and focus on key details

MIT developed a novel system utilizing two language models for improving robotic task execution. The first language model's function is to interpret vague human instructions into clearer commands. Subsequently, the second model helps robots filter out extraneous or irrelevant details from those complex instructions. This dual-model approach enhances robot understanding, allowing them to focus precisely on key actions required by the user. The technology aims to make robots more capable assistants in domestic and industrial settings.

People want robots in warehouses and factories, not hospitals or schools, Hexagon study finds

Hexagon's global Robot Generation study reveals public acceptance of robots in workplaces, with both adults and children showing openness to their use. Adults generally expressed a preference for robots performing physical or repetitive tasks. However, the report indicates that people are setting specific boundaries regarding appropriate robot deployment locations. Notably, respondents showed less enthusiasm or cited reservations about using robots in sensitive environments like hospitals or schools. Overall, public desire favors robots augmenting industrial and factory settings rather than care or educational ones.
