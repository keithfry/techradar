ASML's Giant Machine Powers Future Microchip Revolution

ASML, a Dutch company vital to the microchip industry, manufactures advanced lithography machines used to pattern minuscule circuitry onto silicon wafers. Verifiable Foundation Models for Robot Safety

Deploying large foundation models for robot control is challenging due to their opacity, which complicates formal verification despite their high expressive power. The paper introduces FEARL (Foundation-Enabled Assured Robot Learning), a framework addressing this by modularly decomposing the policy. FEARL separates a powerful Controller ($\text{C}$) from a small Safety module ($\text{S}$). $\text{S}$ operates on low-dimensional sensor observations and bounded context from $\text{C}$, enabling formal verification of critical safety constraints. Evaluation across simulated domains and physical robots demonstrates that this design preserves task capability while ensuring verifiable safety guarantees.

Enforcing Human-like Kinematics in Dexterous Piano Playing via Adversarial Posture Regularization

Reinforcement learning for bimanual piano playing often produces unnatural hand postures with high degrees of freedom. The proposed method, Adversarial Posture Regularization (APR), addresses this by using a small amount of casual human data instead of expensive expert demonstrations. APR incorporates an adversarial objective that forces the trained policy's posture distribution to match a learned human prior. The researchers also collected and released unstructured hand motion data from Meta Quest 3 users playing piano, retargeting it for the Shadow Hand. This approach significantly improves visual quality and adherence to human kinematics across multiple metrics compared to previous techniques.

arXiv - cs.RO

Topological Online Learning for Displacement-based Formation Control

Introduces Topological Online Learning for Displacement-based (TOLD) control, a real-time framework that adapts interaction topology weights directly to minimize formation distortion. TOLD proposes two methods: Online Gradient Flow (OGF) with unconstrained weights and Online Exponential Gradient Flow (OExpGF) with non-negative convex weights. Theoretical analysis proves OExpGF ensures asymptotic consensus for single-integrator agents on directed graphs, while OGF guarantees bounded distortion. Simulations show significant reductions in Root Mean Distortion Error when TOLD is combined with node-level controllers. Hardware testing using Crazyflie quadrotors confirmed substantial improvements in median formation distortion compared to fixed-weight methods.

arXiv - cs.RO

SPACE: Enabling Learning from Cross-Robot Data Toward Generalist Policies

Learning generalizable robot policies by scaling data across diverse robots faces challenges because actions are specific to the collecting robot's dynamics. To solve this, a new framework called SPACE uses Cartesian state delta as a universal action representation for all robots. SPACE operates in three domains: varying embodiments, different hardware units of one embodiment, and dynamic changes during operation. The framework comprises a Cartesian state delta policy predicting geometric end-effector displacement and an Action Adapter converting this into robot-specific commands. Experiments demonstrate that SPACE significantly outperforms direct control command prediction across varied robotics setups and maintains robustness to deployment dynamics shifts.

arXiv - cs.RO

MinInter: Minimizing Trajectory Interpolation During Data Augmentation for Imitation Learning

Imitation learning's data collection costs are addressed by trajectory-level data augmentation, which recombines expert demonstrations with varied initial states. Existing methods often introduce non-expert transitions through interpolation, potentially lowering data quality. Minimizing Interpolation (MinInter) introduces a selection method that chooses the source demonstration requiring the least interpolation for each sampled initial configuration. By explicitly minimizing interpolations during generation, MinInter creates higher-quality synthetic demonstrations compatible with current frameworks. Experiments on 12 tasks demonstrated MinInter consistently improves both data and policy success rates compared to methods like SkillGen.

DynaWM: Dynamics-Aware Distillation with World Model and Momentum Targets for Smooth Locomotion over Continuous Stairs

DynaWM is a novel framework designed to improve bipedal-wheeled robot locomotion over continuous stairs, addressing limitations in current teacher-student control methods. It introduces a world model regularizer to enhance terrain encoding by enforcing forward-dynamics awareness and enabling visualization of terrain geometry. Furthermore, a momentum target encoder stabilizes knowledge transfer by providing consistent distillation targets against non-stationary updates. Analysis shows that the learned representations hierarchically capture terrain geometry with improved adaptability and smoothness. Experiments in both simulation and real hardware confirm DynaWM's superior performance on diverse continuous staircases.

arXiv - cs.RO

NavWM: A Unified Navigation World Model for Foresight-Driven Planning

Navigating complex environments presents challenges for conventional policies due to myopic decision-making and mode collapse. Existing world models often separate perception, generation, and control components inappropriately. NavWM proposes a unified model integrating latent world reasoning, multimodal action prediction, and controllable visual generation. It utilizes latent world tokens to encode geometric and semantic priors for structural understanding. This framework employs an anchor-based forecasting method generating diverse actions, enabling the model to function as a robust planner using visual foresight.
