Interview with Digid’s Nils Könne and Christian Kreil: Nanoscale sensors could help solve robotics’ tactile sensing challenge

Effective operation of robots in physical environments requires sophisticated environmental understanding beyond just software or processing power. This necessity has spurred significant interest in advanced sensing technologies for robotics. Nanoscale sensors are emerging as a key solution to address the current challenge of tactile sensing in machines. According to Nils Könne and Christian Kreil, these nanoscale sensors show promise for improving robotic interaction with the world. Developing such advanced physical sensing capabilities is crucial for the advancement of practical robotics applications.

Interview with Clearpath Robotics co-founder Ryan Gariepy: ‘Most industries in Canada are under-automated’

Despite a strong national reputation for robotics research through universities and startups, Canadian industry adoption lags in practice. Ryan Gariepy, a co-founder of Clearpath Robotics, notes that most industries across Canada remain under-automated. This suggests a gap between advanced technological capacity and real-world industrial implementation. The technology has shown potential application across sectors like manufacturing, logistics, agriculture, mining, and defense. Addressing this gap is key to translating research strengths into broader economic deployment.

Interview with Sharpa’s Alicia Veneziani: ‘Dexterous manipulation is the key to useful humanoid robots’

Locomotion capabilities, such as walking and running, are garnering significant attention in humanoid robotics research. However, many experts suggest that advanced movement is only a partial hurdle. The greater challenge for creating truly useful humanoid robots involves more familiar human abilities. Dexterous manipulation skills are identified by some researchers as the key area needing major advancement. This suggests fine motor control over mobility may be the current bottleneck in field utility.
