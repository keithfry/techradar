AI 101: From Tokens to Answers: What Actually Happens During LLM Inference

Inference in large language models (LLMs) has evolved into a complex system with multiple mechanisms working behind the scenes. The process includes tokenization, embeddings, attention, KV cache, request routing, batching, memory management, and optimization pipelines. During LLM inference, the model processes requests in two phases: prefill and decode. In prefill, the model reads the entire prompt and builds understanding of context, while in decode, it generates responses one token at a time, with the first output token taking the longest to process. The total latency is determined by the Time to First Token (TTFT) and Time per Output Token (TPOT), with prefill requiring more compute and decode being memory-bandwidth-bound.
