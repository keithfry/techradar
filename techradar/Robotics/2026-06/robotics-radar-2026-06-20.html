ALS Patient Speaks With Brain Implant Breakthrough

Casey Harrell, who has ALS and is paralyzed, has been using an implanted brain-computer interface (BCI) for nearly three years. The system records activity from the speech motor cortex to translate thought into spoken words. Since becoming proficient, Harrell can largely operate the device independently to communicate, surf the web, and work. Within 22.6 months post-implantation, he logged over 3,800 hours of use at home without research staff present. This breakthrough allows individuals like Harrell to regain substantial abilities lost due to degenerative diseases.

ROS Discourse General: ZeroDDS: a pure-Rust RMW for ROS 2 (rc.3), built against 349 real ROS↔DDS pain reports

ZeroDDS is an alternative Robot Middleware (RMW) for ROS 2 written entirely in memory-safe Rust, designed to address 349 reported ROS↔DDS pain points. It functions as a drop-in replacement and demonstrates bidirectional interoperability with existing RMW implementations like CycloneDDS. Key features include fixing silent QoS mismatches and improving WiFi discovery stability through initial announcement bursts. The current release candidate supports essential ROS 2 functionality, including zero-copy shared memory on Windows. While it matches performance of established stacks, users should test for specific cross-middleware incompatibilities with the development team.
