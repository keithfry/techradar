ROS Discourse General: STS3215 pan/tilt + LD19: a no-SDK 3D scanning module for ROS 2 Jazzy

A fully functional ROS 2 subsystem converts a pan/tilt platform and a 2D LiDAR into a no-SDK 3D scanner. This setup uses Feetech STS3215 servos to aim an LDROBOT LD19, with a node assembling 2D scans into `PointCloud2` using the live TF tree. The system is designed to function purely in ROS 2 or optionally communicate via MQTT for external control. It addresses technical hurdles like fixing an upstream LiDAR driver build issue and bypassing vendor SDKs by implementing serial protocols directly. This entire project is open-sourced under GPL-3.0 and works on platforms like the RK3588 and Pi 5.

The Role of RF Connectors in Robotic Vision, Sensor Communication, and Automated Inspection Systems

RF connectors play a critical, often overlooked role in robotic vision and automated inspection systems. These demanding applications rely on reliable physical signal pathways for high performance. Poor connectivity, such as dirty or vibrating interfaces, can degrade signals regardless of advanced software or powerful processing units. Camera data transmission and sensor communication are particularly vulnerable to these physical disruptions. Consequently, the robustness of RF connectors is paramount for consistent system operation in real-world environments.
